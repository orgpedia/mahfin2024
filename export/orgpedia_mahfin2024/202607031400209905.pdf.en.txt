Introduction - The Finance Department is a very important department of the Government of Maharashtra. Under the provisions of the Constitution, the Finance Department carries out important responsibilities such as preparing the annual budget of the state government and presenting it to the legislature, keeping a close watch on the daily balance of the government in the Reserve Bank of India, taking necessary and expeditious measures to maintain the fiscal position, preparing advance estimates of the deposits and expenditure in the financial management of the state, and providing adequate funds for the implementation of various schemes of development and public welfare of the state. The above mentioned works need to be done more expeditiously and effectively to achieve the target of US $5 trillion economy under Vikas Maharashtra 2047. For this, the government was considering setting up three dedicated cells, namely, ASPIRE, BEACON and BRIDGE for coordination of Centrally Sponsored Schemes, coordination of PSUs and asset monetization and management of loans and guarantees respectively.
The Government's Decision-
The major initiatives to be undertaken by the Department of Finance under the implementation of Vikas Maharashtra 2047 are 1) To create financial provision by restructuring performance-based expenditure and revenue models (models), 2) To increase the availability of alternative capital and promote asset monetization strategy and 3) Public Private Partnership (PPP) and to create a strong institutional framework involving all stakeholders to strengthen the financial planning capacity to control and monitor the flow of funds into the Deposit Expenditure in the Department of Finance for implementation of these initiatives.
ASPIRE (Accessing Schemes and Planning for Influences, Reforms and Expenditure) Cell, BEACON (Benchmarking Enterprise Accountability and Capital Oversight Network) Cell for Public Sector Undertaking Monitoring and Asset Monetization and BRIDGE (Borrowing, Reforms, Investment and Debt Governance Entity Cell for Monitoring Debt Matters)

The functions of these three cells will be as follows.
ASPIRE Cell is being set up to manage the funds received from various Central Government Ministries, 16th Finance Commission and Centrally Sponsored Schemes and the functions of this Cell will be as follows: - To review the Centrally Sponsored Schemes (CSS) at the Finance Division level, to prepare and update the plan-wise data base to control the funds received from the Central Government, State share, PFMS and SNA compliance, utilization of funds and status of utilization certificates.
2. To meet the guidelines of the scheme in coordination with the concerned administrative departments so that maximum funds are received by the state under the Central Government's "Special Assistance to States for Capital Investment Scheme."
3. To regulate the funds received from the Sixteenth Finance Commission, to ensure that the conditions laid down by the Commission are met by the Department.
4. Prepare and submit real-time SATS and monthly reports on funds and expenditure received from various schemes.
5. Full integration with Central PFMS, coordination to ensure compliance of SNA touch system by the respective offices of the administrative divisions.
6. To act as a synergy between the administrative divisions of the Ministry, the Central Ministries, the Finance Department and the Office of the Accountant General (AG).
BEACON (Benchmarking Enterprise Accountability and Capital Oversight Network) A BEACON Cell is being set up for asset monetization, disinvestment, restructuring, etc., with supervision, review, and supervision of State Public Undertakings and the functions of this Cell will be as follows: - 1. 'Strategic', 'Viable / Revital', and 'Viable / Passive' of PSUs. Establishing a framework for classification into such categories.
2. Creation of dashboards for annual review of various ratios of financial position of PSUs 3. Check creditworthiness of PSUs seeking guarantees; Recommending risk-based guarantee charges consistent with the rules of the 16th Finance Commission.

To quantify the risk of public undertakings through guarantees, groot, equity, and accrual grants; to prepare an annual contingency liability statement.

3. BRIDGE (Borrowing, Reforms, Investment and Debt Governance Entity) A BRIDGE Cell is being set up to regulate all matters relating to government and open market borrowings as well as to comprehensively manage matters relating to loan guarantees.
Review of Detailed Project Report (DPR) submitted by the Department; Assessment of Financial Feasibility, NPV, IRR and Repayment Capacity.
Participation in loan negotiations with organizations such as MDBs, NABARD, and HUDCO; advice on terms, contracts, and currency risk.
Track delivery by physical stages; coordinate with Project Implementation Agencies (PIAs).
Monitoring of debt appropriations by enforcement agencies; coordination with funding agency records.
Creating a repayment schedule; arranging principal and interest payments on payment dates.
Conduct periodic debt repayment analysis (DSA); monitor rollovers, interest rates, exchange rates, and operating risks.
Create a benchmark issue policy, prepare a quarterly / annual debt calendar, and analyze market response.
